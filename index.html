Shihao Fu

I am a PhD candidate in the Faculty of Civil Engineering and Geosciences at Delft University of Technology, The Netherlands. My research focuses on the evolution of rock salt fracture geometry and mechanical behavior during fluid–rock interactions.

I investigate dissolution, transport, and healing processes in rock salt through laboratory experiments and finite element simulations. My work combines flow-through experiments, long-term healing tests, image-based fracture characterization, and constitutive modeling implemented via Fortran subroutines in ABAQUS.

I received my B.S. and M.S. degrees in Petroleum Engineering from China University of Petroleum (Beijing) and Yangtze University. My previous work focused on hydraulic fracture initiation and propagation in coal measure shale reservoirs.

Research

Sealing and healing behaviour of rock salt fractures

My PhD project investigates how rock salt fractures dissolve, evolve, and heal under varying chemical and mechanical conditions. Using flow-through visualization experiments, I study transitions between diffusion- and advection-dominated regimes. Detailed image processing (MATLAB, Avizo, MeshLab) and 3D surface reconstruction (SolidWorks) are used to quantify aperture changes and interface roughness.

Long-term healing experiments (up to one year) are conducted to measure shear strength recovery, internal friction angle, and stick–slip behavior using direct shear tests. On the modelling side, I extend an elastic–plastic constitutive model incorporating healing, implemented through user-defined Fortran subroutines in ABAQUS, and use COMSOL for reactive-transport processes in evolving fracture geometries.

Hydraulic fracturing in coal measure shale reservoirs

My Master's research involved true triaxial hydraulic fracturing experiments on shale–coal interbedded samples, studying how lithological layering and in-situ stress contrasts influence fracture propagation. I also developed a 3D hydraulic fracture model in ABAQUS using a coupled seepage–stress–damage cohesive framework.
