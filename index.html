About Me

I am Shihao Fu, a dedicated fourth-year PhD student in Applied Geophysics and Petrophysics based in the Netherlands. My academic journey bridges medicine and geophysics, with a Bachelor's and Master's degree in medicine from China, followed by specialized research in numerical modeling and experimental work. My PhD research focuses on the dissolution and healing dynamics of rock salt fractures, combining innovative experimental approaches with advanced numerical simulations using methods like finite element analysis. I've explored topics such as fluid flow, solute transport, and the degradation and recovery of materials under stress, contributing valuable insights to both academia and industry.

Throughout my academic career, I have developed strong analytical skills and a results-driven mindset, balancing my tasks with strategic planning. I regularly present my findings at international conferences and collaborate with esteemed researchers, including Anne Pluymakers and David Bruhn, on cutting-edge projects in reservoir engineering and geomechanics.

Beyond research, I am committed to sharing knowledge and engaging with diverse audiences. I aim to inspire future researchers and contribute to advancements in geophysics and engineering.
