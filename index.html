About Me

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in Applied Geophysics and Petrophysics at Delft University of Technology. My research focuses on the evolution, sealing, and healing of rock salt fractures during fluid–rock interactions, with applications to the design of safe and reliable underground energy storage systems.

My work combines laboratory experiments and numerical modelling. Experimentally, I perform flow-through visualization tests, long-term healing experiments, and mechanical testing (direct shear, triaxial compression, fracture toughness). Numerically, I use finite element simulations in ABAQUS (with user-defined Fortran subroutines) and COMSOL to study reactive transport and mechanical–chemical coupling.

I hold a Bachelor’s and Master’s degree in Petroleum Engineering from China, where I worked on hydraulic fracture initiation and propagation in coal measure shale reservoirs. I actively collaborate with international research groups and regularly present at conferences on rock mechanics, geo-energy and coupled THMC processes.

I am broadly interested in rock mechanics, underground energy storage, and coupled processes in fractured porous media, and I enjoy bridging experimental observations with numerical models.

Research

Study of Sealing and Healing Behaviour of Rock Salt Fractures

PhD Project | TU Delft

This project investigates how rock salt fractures dissolve, seal, and heal under different fluid and stress conditions, with relevance to underground energy storage and subsurface integrity. I design and perform flow-through experiments to study transitions between diffusion- and advection-dominated regimes, quantify fracture geometry evolution, and link these changes to mechanical properties such as shear strength and stick–slip behaviour.

On the modelling side, I implement an elastic–plastic constitutive model with healing behaviour in ABAQUS using user-defined Fortran subroutines, and perform reactive-transport simulations in COMSOL based on image-derived geometries.

Rock salt Fracture healing Image-based modelling ABAQUS & COMSOL Fortran (UMAT/UEL)

Fracture Initiation and Propagation in Coal Measure Shale

MSc Project | China University of Petroleum, Beijing

This work combined mechanical testing and true triaxial hydraulic fracturing experiments to study fracture propagation in coal measure shale. I analysed the impact of lithological layering and in-situ stress contrasts on fracture height growth and geometry, and developed a 3D hydraulic fracture model in ABAQUS using a coupled seepage–stress–damage approach with a Cohesive Zone Model.
