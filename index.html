About Me

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in Applied Geophysics and Petrophysics at Delft University of Technology. My background is in Petroleum Engineering, with both Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees from China. I work at the interface of rock mechanics, hydrodynamics, and numerical modelling.

My PhD research focuses on the evolution of rock salt fracture geometry and mechanical properties during fluid–rock interactions. I combine flow-through visualization experiments, long-term healing tests, and finite element simulations to study how dissolution, transport, and healing processes control fracture sealing and shear strength recovery in rock salt.

The findings support the design of safer and more reliable rock salt caverns for large-scale underground energy storage and carbon storage, contributing to the energy transition. I regularly present my work at international conferences and collaborate with an interdisciplinary team at TU Delft and partner institutions.

Research

Sealing and Healing Behaviour of Rock Salt Fractures

PhD Project | TU Delft

This project investigates how rock salt fractures dissolve, evolve, and heal under different chemical and mechanical conditions. I perform flow-through visualization experiments to study fluid concentration, flow rate, and initial geometry effects on the transition between diffusion- and advection-dominated regimes in fracture dissolution. Image processing (MATLAB, Avizo, MeshLab) and 3D surface reconstruction (SolidWorks) are used to quantify geometry changes.

I also conduct long-term healing experiments (up to one year), followed by direct shear tests to evaluate shear strength recovery, internal friction angle, and stick–slip behaviour. On the numerical side, I extend an elastic–plastic constitutive model with healing behaviour, implemented via user-defined Fortran subroutines in ABAQUS, and use COMSOL for reactive-transport simulations.

Flow-through experiments Direct shear tests Image processing ABAQUS (UMAT/UEL) COMSOL Fortran

Previous Research: Hydraulic Fracturing in Coal Measure Shales

M.S. Project | China University of Petroleum, Beijing

During my Master’s, I studied fracture initiation and propagation in coal measure shale reservoirs using true triaxial hydraulic fracturing experiments on 30 × 30 × 30 cm blocks. I investigated how lithological layering and in-situ stress differences influence fracture geometry, and developed a 3D hydraulic fracture model in ABAQUS using a coupled seepage–stress–damage approach with a Cohesive Zone Model.
