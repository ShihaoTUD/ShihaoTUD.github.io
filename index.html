I am a PhD candidate in Applied Geophysics and Petrophysics at TU Delft, with my defense scheduled for February 26, 2026. My research focuses on the evolution of rock salt fracture geometry and mechanical properties during fluid-rock interactions, combining laboratory experiments and numerical modeling to advance understanding of fracture dissolution, transport, and healing processes.

My work contributes to the design of safer and more reliable rock salt caverns for large-scale energy storage, supporting the energy transition. I investigate how rock salt fractures dissolve, evolve, and heal under varying chemical and mechanical conditions through flow-through visualization experiments, long-term healing tests, and advanced constitutive modeling implemented via Fortran subroutines in ABAQUS and COMSOL.

I received my M.S. in Petroleum Engineering from China University of Petroleum (Beijing) and my B.S. from Yangtze University. My previous work focused on hydraulic fracture initiation and propagation in coal measure shale reservoirs.
